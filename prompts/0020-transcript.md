# Summarize a lecture or video transcript

Below is a transcript of a lecture, talk, or video — usually a single
speaker. The text was likely machine-transcribed and may contain
disfluencies, filler words, and recognition errors. Read past those.

Produce:

- One sentence describing the topic and the speaker's purpose.
- The main ideas in the order the speaker presents them, with brief
  definitions of any new terms they introduce.
- Concrete examples, demos, or studies referenced along the way.
- Closing takeaways, recommendations, or open questions.

Prefer the speaker's own framing over a reorganization of your own.
Skip filler ("um", "you know"), meta-talk ("welcome to today's
session", "as I said earlier"), and obvious transcription errors.
